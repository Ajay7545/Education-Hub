PHYSICS VIVA QUESTION




CAREY FOSTER BRIDGE



It was invented by George Carey Foster


Q1.What is resistance?

A.Resistance R=V \ I, where V is the potential difference applied across the wire and I is the current flowing
through it due to the potential difference.

Q2.What is specific resistance?

A.Specific resistance of a material is the resistance of one centimeter length of wire whose cross-sectional
area is one square centimeter.

Q3.What is the effect of temperature on resistance?

A.Resistance increases with increase in temperature of the material of wire.

Q4.In what materials the resistance decreases with increase in temperature?

A.In Semiconductors

Q5. If the radius of wire is doubled will the specific resistance change?

A.Specific resistance is independent of length or radius of wire it only depends on the material of the wire.

Q6.Why is the resistance wire wire doubled before winding over the bobbin?

A.To avoid the inductive effects. The direction of current in one wire is opposite to the other hence the total
flux is always zero.

Q7.What is the principle of Carey Foster Bridge?

A.Position of null point changes on the wire when the resistance is connected in the outer gaps is interchanged.
The difference in the value of resistance is equal to the resistances of the differences of bridge wire i.e (L-l).

Q8.What is the principle of Wheatstone Bridge?

A.When the four resistances P,Q,R,S in the four arms of the Wheatstone bridge are such that there is no
deflection in the galvanometer, we have:
P/Q=R/S

Q9.When is Carey Foster Bridge most sensitive?

A.It is most sensitive when P,Q,R,S are same or of the same order.

Q10.Why the resistance for inner ratio arms be equal?

A.For increased sensitivity and accuracy.

Q11.What is the minimum difference in resistances that can be measured by Carey Foster Bridge?

A.The resistance of one millimeter length of bridge wire.
