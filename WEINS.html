PHYSICS VIVA QUESTION




WEIN's LAW




Q1.Define Wien’s distribution law.

A. Wien’s approximation also called Wien’s distribution law is a law of physics used to describe the spectrum of thermal radiation frequently called the blackbody function.

Q2.Define Wien’s displacement law.

A. Wien’s displacement law states that the black body radiation curve for different temperatures peaks at a wavelength inversely proportional to the temperature.

Q3.What is a black body?

A. A black body is one that absorbs all incident electromagnetic radiations and once it reaches thermal equilibrium emits electromagnetic radiations called black body
radiations.

Q4.What kind of solar cell is used?

A.Photo-voltaic cell- Celliniun type.

Q5.Define photo-voltaic effect.

A.The “photovoltaic effect” is the basic physical process through which a solar cell converts sunlight into electricity

Q6.Why do we use different filters?

A.So as to allow light of only certain frequency to pass.

Q7.What is the value of Boltzman constant?

A. kB= 1.387 x 10^-23 Joules/Kelvin

Q8.What is Planck’s law?

A.Planck’s law describes the electromagnetic radiation emitted by a black body in thermal equilibrium at a definite temperature.

Q9.What is Rayleigh-Jeans law?

A. Rayleigh–Jeans law attempts to describe the spectral radiance of electromagnetic radiation at all wavelengths from a black body at a given temperature through classical
arguments.

Q10.What was so special that Planck’s law overrode wein’s and Rayleigh’s?

A. Lord Rayleigh‘s hypothesis may be used to accurately describe the long wavelength spectrum of thermal radiation but fails to describe the short wavelength spectrum of
thermal emission,similarly Wein’s law is true for short wavelength but fails for long wavelengths.But Planck’s law is true for the entire spectrum.

Q11.Define Draper current.

A.It is the minimum current required to just ignite the filament of the bulb.

Q12.Why do we assume that the bulb is a black body?

A.This is so because the condition to create a black body artificially is to enclose the light source in a container and provide a single thin & narrow outlet of light.

Q13.What is the standard value of planck’s constant?

A. h = 6.626 × 10-34 Joule-sec

Q14.Can we use photoelectric effect in place of this method to calculate Planck’s constant?

A.Yes,we can use it because in both light energy is converted to electrical energy.
